Speed Through Flawless Precision

Locating your ancestors' documents is only the first half of the battle. The second half is the official administrative proceeding known as the Confirmation of Polish Citizenship. This formal application is adjudicated by the Polish Provincial Governor (Voivodeship) and demands an uncompromising adherence to civil code.

In Poland, bureaucracy moves exactly as fast as your paperwork allows. If a dossier is submitted with a missing international Apostille, an uncertified translation, or a formatting error, the government will issue a formal request for evidence, effectively freezing your case for 6 to 12 months.

My VIP Verification service is engineered to eliminate these delays entirely. I perform a rigorous pre-filing audit to ensure your legal dossier is 100% flawless before it ever reaches a government desk.

All-Inclusive Premium Representation

Applying for citizenship from abroad often forces applicants to navigate confusing consulate appointments, international courier risks, and complex language barriers. I act as your official legal proxy (Pełnomocnik) directly on the ground in Poland.

By retaining my VIP services, you secure:

  • Direct Warsaw Filing: I bypass the heavily backlogged embassy system and file your application directly with the government authorities in Poland.
  • In-House Sworn Translations: The Polish government strictly requires foreign vital records to be translated by a state-registered Polish Sworn Translator. I manage all required translations seamlessly within my agency.
  • Complete Case Management: All official government correspondence is legally routed to my office. If the authorities have a legislative inquiry, I submit the legal response on your behalf.

The 3-Point Eligibility Test

Before we execute the archival process, we must establish a foundational legal baseline. You are highly likely to qualify for our VIP Fast-Track if you can answer "Yes" to these three criteria:

  • ☑ Your ancestor was born in Poland or held Polish citizenship.
