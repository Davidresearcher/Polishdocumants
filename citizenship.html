Confirmation of Polish Citizenship by Descent

The acquisition of Polish citizenship by descent is an intricate legal process governed by the foundational principle of jus sanguinis, ensuring that the right of blood transcends geographic boundaries. For the global Jewish diaspora, the process is a formal confirmation of a pre-existing legal status.

This procedure requires an exhaustive reconstruction of family lineage and a rigorous analysis of the shifting legislative landscape from 1920 to the present day.

The Military Paradox and Disqualification

One of the most complex aspects is the "Military Paradox," derived from Article 11 of the 1920 Act. It created a protection for male citizens: acquisition of foreign nationality was only recognized by Poland if a formal exemption from military duty was granted. Conversely, actual service in a foreign military (e.g., the IDF) prior to 1951 remains a primary disqualifier.
