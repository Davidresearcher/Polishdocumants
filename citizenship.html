The Military Paradox and Disqualification

One of the most complex aspects is the "Military Paradox," derived from Article 11 of the 1920 Act. It created a protection for male citizens: acquisition of foreign nationality was only recognized by Poland if a formal exemption from military duty was granted. Conversely, actual service in a foreign military (e.g., the IDF) prior to 1951 remains a primary disqualifier.
